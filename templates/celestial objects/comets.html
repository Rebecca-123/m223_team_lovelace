Comets

To be honest, you can think of Comets as a bigger type of snowball. Except much bigger. Think gigantic.

It's composed of rocks, gases, and dust that orbit around the Sun. A good estimate for the size of a comet would be a small town.

As the comet's orbit brings the comet closer and closer to the Sun, it heats up which causes the comet to burst open and turn into dust.

The dust and gases released from the Comet form a "giant glowing head" that is larger than most planets.

To give an estimate, there are only about 3743 known Comets, but scientists predict that there maybe billions more.
