Mercury is the smallest planet of the Solar System, and the closest one to the sun. Its lack of atmosphere means that its temperature can vary wildly from daytime to nighttime.

Venus is the second planet from the sun, and has the hottest surface temperature due to its runaway greenhouse gas effect. It is the brightest object in the sky besides the moon and sun, and has the longest rotation period of any planet.

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbour and support life. About 29.2% of Earth's surface is land consisting of continents and islands. The remaining 70.8% is covered with water Earth's atmosphere consists mostly of nitrogen and oxygen. More solar energy is received by tropical regions than polar regions and is redistributed by atmospheric and ocean circulation.

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun, and the last of the terrestrial planets. It has long been the subject of science fiction and investigation through spacecraft. It is orbited by two moons, Phobos and Deimos, most likely captured asteroids. Its red hue is caused by oxidation in the martian soil, and Mars is the site of the biggest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons.

The largest planet in the solar system, Jupiter's gravity is theorized to disrupt the paths of comets and asteroids that would otherwise have endangered Earth. It consists of mainly hydrogen and helium, and is orbited by 79 natural satellites. Its most prominent features include The Great Red Spot- a storm that has been slowly decreasing in size in recent years, a faint ring system, and strong magnetosphere.

Saturn is easily recognizable by its distinct ring system and has the most natural satellites of any planet in the solar system, at 82. Its color is caused by ammonia crystals and it has very high wind speeds.

Uranus' rotational axis is tilted by around 90 degrees, meaning that its poles lie where other planets' equators would be. True color photographs show that Uranus' cloudtops are nearly featureless, and the planet has a dark ring system tilted the same way as its rotational axis. Its color is caused by methane gas.

Neptune is the eighth and farthest-known Solar planet from the Sun. In the Solar System, it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet.
