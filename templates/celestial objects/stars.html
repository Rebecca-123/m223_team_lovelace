Introduction

Stars defined our study of the night sky. Our ancestors looked to the sky but they couldn't make out faint galaxies, theorize about black holes, or conduct astrophotometrical experiments to verify the potential existence of exoplanets, but they could see stars in the night as sure as they could see the sun during the day. The sun itself is a star, though it is much closer and thus its apparent magnitude (the measure of how bright something is to us) is far greater. Well, actually it would be lower as lower magnitudes are brighter, but you get the idea.

Stars come in one shape and a lot of sizes.
